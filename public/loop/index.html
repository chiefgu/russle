01 · colour

Bloom. warm paper and a terracotta red.

Went with warm paper and a terracotta red instead of full pink. Gets us closer to that sheerluxe editorial feel you wanted without tipping into beauty brand. Warmer than plain black and white, a bit quieter than a straight red, and the espresso text keeps it grown up.

Blush and sand are the two softer tones for alt sections and badges, so the site doesn't end up all one colour. Everything you're looking at on this page is already in the palette, so what you see is basically what you'd get.

02 · typography

Newsreader for headings, Manrope for everything else.

Newsreader's the serif. Feels more like an indie magazine than a wedding invite, which is what we want. It'll do the hero headlines and any italic pullquotes from your manifesto. Manrope handles everything else, body copy, buttons, nav, forms. Clean but not cold.

Both are free from google fonts so no faff with licensing. This whole page is set in them, so you're reading the actual thing. The italic you're reading right now is how the manifesto italics would look.

03 · wordmark

The italic l stayed, the rest is upright.

Kept the italic l since you liked it. Paired it with a normal upright oop. The swash on the l does the character bit, the oop keeps it readable. Little gap after the l so the swash has room to breathe into the first o without crashing into it. Instrument Serif throughout.

Works on primary, on paper, on espresso. Three grounds we'd actually use in production below.

04 · app icon · monogram

Two stacked o's for the square spaces.

For anywhere the full word doesn't fit, app icon, insta profile pic, favicon, I've gone with the two rotated o's stacked. They overlap enough that it doesn't look like a figure 8, and the rotation kills any remaining infinity vibes. Double-o is kinda the motif now, there's a loop literally inside the letters.

Four grounds first, then a quick mock of how it'd look on your phone, in a browser tab, and as an insta profile pic.
