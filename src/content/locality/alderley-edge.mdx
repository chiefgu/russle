## A village high street that judges by appearance.

Alderley Edge is small, dense, and unforgiving. London Road runs the spine of it, and almost every business that matters lives within a few minutes of that stretch. Customers walk in or drive in, and the decision to step through your door is made before anyone has read a word. Brand quality, signage, window, website. Each one is a small handshake, and a weak handshake costs you the customer.

That changes the brief for every business that opens here. The bakery, the deli, the salon, the wine merchant, the boutique, the family-run restaurant. The fundamentals are the same as any independent business anywhere, but the bar is higher because the audience is sharper. People who shop in Alderley Edge spend confidently when something feels right, and quietly walk past when it does not.

We work with that buyer in mind. The brand has to be specific enough to feel like itself, and good enough to belong on London Road next to anything. The website has to load instantly, look like the photography was shot for it, and make booking or buying take three taps. The local SEO has to put the business in the map pack when someone is standing on the pavement looking up "[your category] near me".

When the brand and the site agree with each other, and both agree with the room the business sits in, the rest of growth starts working without being forced. That is the order we build in.
